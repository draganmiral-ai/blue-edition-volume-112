SAFFRON

Edition / Volume 112

To My Imaginary Lover

Tonight,
I met you at a red light.

Not in a dream,
nor in some place
designed for romance,
but at an ordinary intersection
while the city carried on
as though nothing sacred
were happening above it.

The light told me to wait.

So I waited.

And somewhere between
the traffic signals,
the passing cars,
and the evening sun,
the sky opened
and reminded me of you.

The clouds stretched across the heavens
like the intricate texture of your soul,
each one carefully placed,
each one carrying evidence
that God still creates beauty
with intention.

Until the day
you are no longer imagined,
I will continue looking upward.

At every sunset.
At every unexpected light.
At every ordinary place
that suddenly feels sacred.

And when the road finally opens,
when the signal changes,
when God decides
we have waited long enough,

perhaps you will be there
on the other side of the crossing,

looking at the same sky,
recognizing me too.

To my imaginary lover,
tonight the light was red,
but the sky promised
it would not remain so forever.

S E

EMERALD

Edition / Volume 112

Something in me stays.

I return before the noise decides.

What is rooted does not rush.

The unseen still holds.

I know where to find myself.

I take the quiet with me.
